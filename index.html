UN Goal

The ocean covers over 70% of the earth's surface and provides a home for countless species of plants and animals. It is the source of life for billions of people around the world, but unfortunately, it is also under threat. In 2015, the United Nations established 17 Sustainable Development Goals, or SDGs, to address the most pressing issues facing our planet. One of these goals, SDG 6, focuses on ensuring access to clean water and sanitation for all. But this goal is about more than just human access to water - it also includes protecting marine life and their habitats. In this scrollytelling story, we will explore the current state of sea creatures and the potential future consequences if we do not take action to protect them.
The world's oceans are facing unprecedented challenges due to human activities. Overfishing, pollution, climate change, and habitat destruction are just some of the major threats to marine life. Every year, an estimated 100 million sharks are killed for their fins, and plastic waste is choking our oceans and harming marine life. Oil spills and chemical pollution are also destroying coral reefs and other crucial habitats, leaving many species on the brink of extinction. The impacts of these threats are already being felt by many sea creatures. Some populations of fish, turtles, and whales have declined by over 90% in just the past few decades. Coral reefs, which are home to a quarter of all marine species, are disappearing at an alarming rate. And as the ocean's temperature rises, more and more species are struggling to survive.
If we do not take action to protect our oceans and the creatures that live there, the consequences could be catastrophic. The loss of key species could have a ripple effect throughout the entire ocean ecosystem, leading to the collapse of fisheries and the loss of important sources of food for people around the world. Without healthy coral reefs, storm surges could devastate coastal communities, and the loss of biodiversity could result in the loss of potential medical breakthroughs and other benefits.
